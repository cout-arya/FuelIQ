CHICKEN DISHES
═══════════════════════════════════════

Chicken Curry — Home (1 serving, 150g bone-in):
Cal: 220 | P: 22g | C: 6g | F: 12g
Notes: Onion-tomato gravy. Most common Indian non-veg dish.

Butter Chicken / Murgh Makhani (1 serving, restaurant):
Cal: 350 | P: 25g | C: 10g | F: 24g
Notes: Cream + butter + tomato. Most ordered restaurant dish in India.
Home version: ~280 kcal (less cream).

Chicken Tikka Masala (1 serving):
Cal: 330 | P: 28g | C: 10g | F: 20g
Notes: Grilled chicken in spiced gravy.

Kadai Chicken (1 serving):
Cal: 280 | P: 26g | C: 8g | F: 16g
Notes: With capsicum, onion in kadai masala. Less creamy.

Chicken Do Pyaza (1 serving):
Cal: 260 | P: 24g | C: 10g | F: 14g
Notes: Double onion preparation. Mughlai origin.

Chicken Korma (1 serving, restaurant):
Cal: 380 | P: 24g | C: 12g | F: 28g
Notes: Cashew-cream based. Mughlai. Very rich.

Chicken Chettinad (1 serving, Tamil Nadu):
Cal: 270 | P: 26g | C: 6g | F: 16g
Notes: Black pepper, fennel, star anise. South Indian spicy.

Chicken Xacuti (1 serving, Goan):
Cal: 290 | P: 24g | C: 8g | F: 18g
Notes: Coconut + poppy seed gravy. Goan Catholic specialty.

Chicken Cafreal (1 serving, Goan):
Cal: 240 | P: 28g | C: 4g | F: 12g
Notes: Green masala marinated. Portuguese-Indian.

Chicken Stew (1 serving, Kerala):
Cal: 200 | P: 22g | C: 10g | F: 8g
Notes: Coconut milk + potato + vegetables. Mild, light.

Chicken Rezala (1 serving, Bengali/Kolkata):
Cal: 300 | P: 24g | C: 6g | F: 20g
Notes: White gravy with yogurt, cashew. Mughlai-Bengali.

Chicken Changezi (1 serving):
Cal: 310 | P: 25g | C: 8g | F: 20g
Notes: Tomato-cashew based. Delhi restaurant specialty.

Tandoori Chicken (1 leg piece, 200g with bone):
Cal: 260 | P: 30g | C: 5g | F: 13g
Notes: Yogurt-marinated, tandoor cooked. No oil cooking. High protein.

Chicken Tikka — Dry (150g boneless):
Cal: 220 | P: 30g | C: 4g | F: 9g
Notes: Best protein-to-calorie ratio. Gym-friendly.

Malai Chicken Tikka (150g):
Cal: 280 | P: 28g | C: 4g | F: 16g
Notes: Cream-marinated. Richer than regular tikka.

Chicken Seekh Kebab (2 pieces):
Cal: 200 | P: 22g | C: 4g | F: 10g
Notes: Minced chicken on skewer. Tandoor cooked.

Chicken Biryani — Home (1 plate, 300g):
Cal: 450 | P: 25g | C: 50g | F: 16g
Notes: Layered rice + chicken with spices.

Chicken Biryani — Hyderabadi Restaurant (1 plate):
Cal: 600 | P: 28g | C: 60g | F: 25g
Notes: Dum-cooked. More ghee and fried onions.

Chicken Biryani — Lucknowi/Awadhi (1 plate):
Cal: 550 | P: 26g | C: 58g | F: 22g
Notes: Pukki biryani style — rice and meat cooked separately.

Chicken Pulao (1 plate):
Cal: 400 | P: 22g | C: 50g | F: 12g
Notes: Lighter than biryani. One-pot method.

Chicken Fried Rice (1 plate, Indo-Chinese):
Cal: 480 | P: 20g | C: 55g | F: 18g
Notes: High sodium. Indo-Chinese restaurant staple.

Chicken Momos — Steamed (6 pieces):
Cal: 250 | P: 16g | C: 28g | F: 8g
Notes: Northeast/Tibetan. Steamed dumplings.

Chicken Momos — Fried (6 pieces):
Cal: 380 | P: 16g | C: 30g | F: 20g
Notes: Deep fried version. Much higher fat.

Chicken Lollipop — Fried (4 pieces):
Cal: 350 | P: 20g | C: 18g | F: 22g
Notes: Indo-Chinese starter. Battered and deep fried.

Chicken 65 (1 serving):
Cal: 320 | P: 22g | C: 15g | F: 18g
Notes: Deep fried spicy chicken. Andhra/Chennai origin.

Chicken Shawarma (1 roll):
Cal: 400 | P: 22g | C: 35g | F: 18g
Notes: With garlic sauce, pickles in rumali roti.

Mutton Nihari (1 serving, Lucknawi):

Haleem (1 bowl, Hyderabadi):

Paya (Trotters soup, 1 bowl):
Cal: 250 | P: 22g | C: 5g | F: 15g

Mutton Seekh Kebab (2 pieces):
Cal: 250 | P: 18g | C: 4g | F: 18g

Galouti Kebab (2 pieces, Lucknawi):

Shammi Kebab (2 pieces):
Cal: 200 | P: 14g | C: 8g | F: 12g
Notes: Chana dal + minced mutton. Flattened, pan-fried.
