DALS, LEGUMES, VEGETABLES & SABZI — ICMR/NIN Reference
Format: Item (portion) | Cal | P | C | F | Fibre | Notes

═══════════════════════════════════════
DALS & LENTILS (per bowl ~200ml cooked, with standard tadka)
═══════════════════════════════════════

Toor Dal / Arhar Dal Tadka (1 bowl):
Cal: 160 | P: 9g | C: 22g | F: 4g | Fibre: 4g
Notes: Most common dal in India. Tadka adds ~40 kcal from ghee/oil.

Moong Dal — Yellow (1 bowl):
Cal: 140 | P: 9g | C: 20g | F: 2g | Fibre: 3g
Notes: Easy to digest. Best for recovery and illness. Low gas.

Moong Dal — Green Whole (1 bowl):
Cal: 155 | P: 10g | C: 22g | F: 2g | Fibre: 5g
Notes: More fibre than yellow. Good for weight loss.

Masoor Dal — Red Lentils (1 bowl):
Cal: 150 | P: 9g | C: 21g | F: 3g | Fibre: 3.5g
Notes: Cooks fastest. Iron-rich. Bengali/UP staple.

Chana Dal (1 bowl):
Cal: 180 | P: 10g | C: 25g | F: 4g | Fibre: 5g
Notes: Low GI (8). Best dal for diabetics.

Urad Dal — Black Gram (1 bowl):
Cal: 170 | P: 10g | C: 23g | F: 4g | Fibre: 4g
Notes: Used in idli/dosa batter. Dense protein.

Dal Makhani (1 bowl, restaurant):
Cal: 320 | P: 12g | C: 28g | F: 18g | Fibre: 5g
Notes: Urad + rajma + cream + butter. Very rich. Home version: ~220 kcal.

Dal Fry (1 bowl):
Cal: 180 | P: 10g | C: 22g | F: 6g | Fibre: 4g
Notes: Mixed dals with onion-tomato tadka. Restaurant staple.

Dal Palak (1 bowl):
Cal: 160 | P: 10g | C: 20g | F: 5g | Fibre: 5g
Notes: Dal cooked with spinach. Iron-rich combination.

Panchmel Dal (1 bowl, Rajasthani):
Cal: 175 | P: 11g | C: 22g | F: 5g | Fibre: 5g
Notes: Five dals mixed. Complete amino acid profile.

Sambhar (1 bowl, South Indian):
Cal: 130 | P: 6g | C: 18g | F: 3g | Fibre: 4g
Notes: Toor dal + vegetables + sambhar powder. Karnataka/TN/Kerala staple.

Rasam (1 bowl):
Cal: 60 | P: 2g | C: 8g | F: 2g | Fibre: 1g
Notes: Thin tamarind-tomato soup. Very low calorie. Digestive aid.

Kadhi — Punjabi (1 bowl):
Cal: 180 | P: 5g | C: 15g | F: 10g | Fibre: 1g
Notes: Yogurt + besan gravy with pakoras. Home version lighter.

Kadhi — Gujarati (1 bowl):
Cal: 120 | P: 4g | C: 12g | F: 6g | Fibre: 1g
Notes: Sweet-sour, thinner than Punjabi. Lower calorie.

Kala Chana (1 bowl):

Lobia / Black-Eyed Peas Curry (1 bowl):

Sundal / Chana Sundal (1 cup, Tamil Nadu):

Usal / Sprouted Moth Curry (1 bowl, Maharashtra):
Cal: 200 | P: 10g | C: 25g | F: 6g | Fibre: 5g

Sprouts Chaat (1 cup):
Cal: 120 | P: 8g | C: 16g | F: 2g | Fibre: 4g
Notes: Mixed sprouts with onion, lemon. High protein snack.

Kadala Curry (1 bowl, Kerala):
Cal: 210 | P: 9g | C: 28g | F: 7g | Fibre: 5g
Notes: Black chickpea curry with coconut. Served with puttu/appam.

Sambar (1 bowl, with vegetables):
Cal: 140 | P: 7g | C: 20g | F: 3g | Fibre: 5g
Notes: Heartier version with drumstick, eggplant, pumpkin.

Dal Dhokli (1 serving, Gujarati):
Cal: 350 | P: 12g | C: 48g | F: 10g | Fibre: 5g
Notes: Wheat dumplings cooked in toor dal. Complete meal.

Pithla / Zunka (1 bowl, Maharashtrian):
Cal: 140 | P: 6g | C: 15g | F: 6g | Fibre: 3g
Notes: Besan (gram flour) gravy. Served with bhakri.
