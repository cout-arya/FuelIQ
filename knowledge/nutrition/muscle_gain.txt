MUSCLE GAIN NUTRITION GUIDELINES — COMPREHENSIVE INDIAN CONTEXT
Source: ISSN Position Stands + ICMR/NIN + Sports Nutrition Research

═══════════════════════════════════════
CALORIC SURPLUS FUNDAMENTALS
═══════════════════════════════════════

SURPLUS STRATEGY:
- Lean bulk: 200-300 kcal above TDEE (minimal fat gain)
- Standard bulk: 300-500 kcal above TDEE (balanced approach)
- Aggressive bulk: 500-700 kcal (faster gains but more fat)
- Beginners can gain muscle at maintenance or slight surplus (newbie gains)
- Target weight gain: 0.25-0.5 kg per week (lean bulk)

ADJUSTING INTAKE:
- If no weight gain after 2 weeks: increase by 200 kcal
- If gaining >0.5 kg/week: reduce by 100 kcal (limiting fat gain)
- Weigh yourself same time weekly (morning, post-bathroom)
- Track waist measurement — if growing faster than chest/arms, reduce surplus

═══════════════════════════════════════
MACRONUTRIENT TARGETS
═══════════════════════════════════════

PROTEIN:
- Target: 1.6-2.2g per kg bodyweight (ISSN recommended)
- 60kg: 96-132g daily | 70kg: 112-154g | 80kg: 128-176g | 90kg: 144-198g
- Higher end (2.0-2.2g) for natural lifters without steroids
- Distribute across 4-5 meals: 25-40g per meal per sitting
- MPS (muscle protein synthesis) peaks at ~0.4g/kg per meal
- Post-workout: 0.3-0.5g/kg bodyweight within 2 hours

CARBOHYDRATES (Fuel for training):
- Target: 4-6g per kg bodyweight
- Training days: 5-6g/kg | Rest days: 3-4g/kg
- Carbs are NOT the enemy for muscle gain — they fuel workouts
- Glycogen-depleted muscles = weaker training = less growth stimulus
- Prioritize complex carbs: roti, brown rice, oats, sweet potato

FATS (Hormonal health):
- Target: 0.8-1.2g per kg bodyweight
- Minimum 20% of total calories
- Critical for testosterone production (low fat = low T)
- Sources: ghee (1-2 tbsp/day), almonds, peanut butter, eggs, fatty fish

MACRO SPLIT:
- Standard: Protein 25% | Carbs 50% | Fat 25%
- High carb day: Protein 25% | Carbs 55% | Fat 20%

═══════════════════════════════════════
MEAL TIMING FOR MUSCLE GAIN
═══════════════════════════════════════

PROTEIN DISTRIBUTION:
- 4-5 meals spaced 3-4 hours apart
- Each meal: 0.3-0.5g protein per kg bodyweight
- Don't exceed 40g per single sitting (diminishing returns)
- "Anabolic window" is 24-48 hours, not just 30 minutes

CRITICAL WINDOWS:
- Pre-workout (1-2 hrs before): Moderate carbs + moderate protein
  → 2 roti + paneer sabzi OR banana + peanut butter + milk
- Post-workout (within 2 hrs): High protein + fast carbs
  → Whey shake + banana OR chicken + rice
- Before bed: Slow-digesting protein (casein, paneer, curd)
  → Prevents overnight muscle protein breakdown (MPS drops during sleep)

TRAINING DAY vs REST DAY:
- Training day: +200-300 kcal surplus, higher carbs
- Rest day: Maintenance or slight surplus (+100-200), moderate carbs
- Protein stays same on both days

═══════════════════════════════════════
SUPPLEMENTS FOR MUSCLE GAIN
═══════════════════════════════════════

ESSENTIAL (Evidence-based):
- Whey protein: If dietary protein is insufficient. 1-2 scoops/day.
- Creatine monohydrate: 5g daily. Proven to increase strength 5-10%.
  No loading phase needed. Just 5g daily with any meal.

HELPFUL:
- Caffeine (pre-workout): 3-6mg/kg bodyweight. Improves performance.
- Vitamin D: 2000-4000 IU if deficient (most Indians are).
  Deficiency impairs muscle recovery and testosterone.
- Omega-3 fish oil: 1-2g EPA+DHA daily. Anti-inflammatory.

NOT NECESSARY:
- Mass gainer: Just eat more food. Mass gainers are sugar + protein.
- BCAAs: Redundant if you eat enough protein.
- Testosterone boosters: No OTC supplement meaningfully raises T.
- Pre-workout powders: Coffee works just as well and is cheaper.

═══════════════════════════════════════
COMMON MISTAKES
═══════════════════════════════════════

- "Dirty bulk" (eating junk for surplus): Gains mostly fat, not muscle
- Skipping breakfast: Missing 1/4 of daily protein
- Only training, ignoring nutrition: Training is the stimulus, food is the builder
- Not eating enough on rest days: Muscle repairs and grows on REST days
- Drinking calories as sugar (juice, soda, sweet lassi): Choose whole foods
- Ignoring vegetables: Micronutrients are essential for recovery
- Not sleeping enough: Growth hormone peaks during deep sleep (10pm-2am)
