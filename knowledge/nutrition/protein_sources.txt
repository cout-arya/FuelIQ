COMPREHENSIVE PROTEIN SOURCES GUIDE — INDIAN CONTEXT
Source: ICMR/NIN + ISSN + PDCAAS/DIAAS Scores

═══════════════════════════════════════
PROTEIN QUALITY SCIENCE
═══════════════════════════════════════

BIOLOGICAL VALUE (BV) — How well body uses the protein:
- Whey protein isolate: BV 104 (highest)
- Whole egg: BV 100 (gold standard)
- Cow's milk: BV 91
- Egg whites: BV 88
- Chicken/Fish: BV 79
- Paneer/Casein: BV 77
- Soy protein: BV 74
- Rice protein: BV 59
- Lentils/Dal: BV 50-65
- Wheat (roti): BV 54

PDCAAS SCORE (Protein Digestibility Corrected Amino Acid Score):
- 1.0 (perfect): Whey, casein, eggs, soy
- 0.9: Chicken, fish, beef
- 0.7: Chickpeas, black beans
- 0.6: Lentils, kidney beans
- 0.5: Peanuts
- 0.4: Wheat

COMPLETE vs INCOMPLETE PROTEINS:
Complete (all 9 essential amino acids): Animal sources, soy, quinoa, hemp, amaranth
Incomplete (missing 1+ amino acids): Most plant sources
Solution: Combine incomplete proteins = complete protein:
- Rice + Dal (lysine + methionine) — the classic Indian combo
- Roti + Chana (wheat + legume)
- Peanut butter + whole wheat bread
- Rice + Rajma

LEUCINE THRESHOLD (Key amino acid for muscle building):
- Need 2.5-3g leucine per meal to trigger MPS
- Whey protein (30g): 3.0g leucine ✓
- Chicken breast (150g): 3.5g leucine ✓
- 3 whole eggs: 1.5g leucine ✗ (need 5-6 eggs)
- Paneer (150g): 2.8g leucine ~✓
- Dal (200g cooked): 1.0g leucine ✗ (need to combine)

VEGETARIAN PROTEIN SOURCES — DETAILED

--- DAIRY-BASED (Highest Quality Vegetarian) ---

Paneer / Cottage Cheese:
Per 100g: Cal 265 | P 18g | C 3g | F 20g
Cost: ₹40-60 per 100g | BV: 77
Best for: High protein meals, tikka, bhurji, gravies
Tip: Low-fat paneer has 20g protein / 180 kcal (better ratio)
Prep: Paneer tikka, bhurji, palak paneer, grilled cubes in salad

Greek Yogurt:
Per 100g: Cal 59 | P 10g | C 3.6g | F 0.4g
Cost: ₹15-25 per 100g | BV: 85
Best for: Breakfast, snack, post-workout
Tip: 3x protein of regular curd. Mix with fruit + honey.
Brands: Epigamia, Nestle a+, Milky Mist

Regular Curd / Dahi:
Per 100g: Cal 60 | P 3g | C 5g | F 3g
Cost: ₹5-8 per 100g
Best for: Daily consumption, raita, lassi
Tip: Probiotic benefits. Low protein per serving though.

Milk — Full Fat:
Per 200ml glass: Cal 120 | P 6g | C 10g | F 6g
Cost: ₹12-15 per 200ml
Toned milk: Cal 90 | P 6g | C 10g | F 3g (better for cutting)
Skimmed milk: Cal 65 | P 6g (best for strict fat loss)

Whey Protein:
Per 30g scoop: Cal 120 | P 24g | C 2g | F 1.5g
Cost: ₹30-50 per serving
BV: 104 (highest of all proteins)
Digestion: 30-60 minutes (fastest)
Best for: Post-workout, breakfast addition, protein target completion
Types:
- Concentrate (WPC 80%): Cheapest, ~80% protein, some lactose
- Isolate (WPI 90%): 90%+ protein, almost zero lactose, pricier
- Hydrolysate: Pre-digested, fastest, most expensive, marginal benefit

Casein Protein:
Per 30g scoop: Cal 120 | P 24g | C 3g | F 1g
Digestion: 4-8 hours (slowest)
Best for: Before bed — sustained amino acid release overnight
Tip: Paneer is a natural casein source

PROTEIN TIMING & DISTRIBUTION SCIENCE
═══════════════════════════════════════

OPTIMAL DISTRIBUTION:
- 4-5 meals, each with 0.3-0.5g protein per kg bodyweight
- 70kg person: 21-35g per meal across 4-5 meals
- Even distribution > front/back-loaded (maximizes MPS peaks)
- Each meal triggers a new MPS peak — 4 peaks > 2 peaks

DAILY SCHEDULE EXAMPLE (150g protein target):
- 7:30 AM Breakfast: 35g (eggs + milk)
- 10:30 AM Snack: 20g (Greek yogurt + almonds)
- 1:00 PM Lunch: 35g (chicken + dal)
- 5:00 PM Pre-workout: 15g (peanut butter + banana)
- 7:30 PM Post-workout: 25g (whey shake)
- 9:30 PM Dinner: 20g (paneer + roti)

SPECIAL POPULATIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════

VEGETARIANS:
- Target: 2.0-2.5g/kg (slightly higher due to lower BV)
- Always combine proteins: dal + rice, roti + chana
- Supplement with whey/casein if possible
- Focus on soy chunks, paneer, Greek yogurt, eggs (if eggetarian)

VEGANS:
- Target: 2.2-2.8g/kg (highest due to plant-only)
- Must combine proteins at every meal
- Soy is the only plant PDCAAS 1.0 source
- Consider plant protein powder (pea + rice blend)
- Supplement B12 (not available in any plant food)

WOMEN:
- Same protein requirements per kg as men
- 1.6-2.0g/kg for general fitness, 2.0-2.4g/kg for muscle gain
- Higher protein especially important during menstrual cycle (luteal phase)
- Don't fear "bulking up" — women lack testosterone for it

OLDER ADULTS (50+):
- Higher protein: 1.2-1.6g/kg minimum (anabolic resistance)
- Leucine threshold higher: need 3-4g per meal (vs 2.5g for young)
- Focus on easily digestible: eggs, fish, dal, whey
