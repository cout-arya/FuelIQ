PRE-WORKOUT & POST-WORKOUT NUTRITION — COMPREHENSIVE INDIAN GUIDE
Source: ISSN Position Stands + ACSM Guidelines + Sports Nutrition Research

═══════════════════════════════════════
PRE-WORKOUT NUTRITION
═══════════════════════════════════════

WHY PRE-WORKOUT NUTRITION MATTERS:
- Glycogen is the primary fuel for resistance training
- Depleted glycogen = 15-30% reduction in work capacity
- Protein before training reduces muscle breakdown during exercise
- Fasted training is acceptable for cardio/fat loss but NOT optimal for strength

TIMING WINDOWS:

2-3 Hours Before (Full Meal):
Macros: 30-50g carbs + 20-30g protein + 10-15g fat
Total: 400-600 kcal
Best for: Morning gym-goers who have time

Indian Options:
- 2 roti + paneer sabzi (light oil) + curd: ~450 kcal, 20g P
- Chicken breast (100g) + rice (1 cup) + dal: ~500 kcal, 30g P
- 3 egg omelette + 2 toast + banana: ~450 kcal, 22g P
- Poha with peanuts + boiled egg: ~350 kcal, 14g P
- Idli (3) + sambhar + boiled egg: ~300 kcal, 16g P
- Oats porridge + whey + banana: ~400 kcal, 30g P
- Rajma (1 small bowl) + 1 roti + curd: ~400 kcal, 16g P
- Upma with vegetables + milk (200ml): ~350 kcal, 14g P
- Pesarattu (2) + coconut chutney + egg: ~350 kcal, 16g P
- Curd rice (1 bowl) + fish fry (1 piece): ~400 kcal, 22g P

1-2 Hours Before (Light Meal):
Macros: 25-40g carbs + 15-20g protein + <10g fat
Total: 200-400 kcal

Indian Options:
- Banana + peanut butter (1 tbsp) + milk: ~300 kcal, 12g P
- 1 roti + paneer (50g): ~250 kcal, 14g P
- Moong dal chilla (1) + curd: ~200 kcal, 12g P
- Poha (small bowl): ~200 kcal, 5g P
- Dates (4) + almonds (10) + milk: ~280 kcal, 10g P
- Thepla (1) + curd: ~200 kcal, 8g P
- Rice cake + peanut butter: ~200 kcal, 6g P

30-60 Minutes Before (Quick Fuel):
Macros: 20-30g fast carbs + minimal fat/fiber
Total: 100-200 kcal

Options:
- Banana (1 medium): 105 kcal — BEST quick pre-workout
- Dates (3-4) + water: 80 kcal — instant energy
- Toast with honey: 120 kcal
- Apple (1 medium): 95 kcal
- Black coffee + banana: 110 kcal — caffeine + carbs combo
- Glucose biscuits (2-3): 80-100 kcal — emergency option
- Sweet potato (small, pre-boiled): 90 kcal
- Mango (1/2 cup, seasonal): 50 kcal

WHAT TO AVOID PRE-WORKOUT:
- Heavy fatty foods (paratha, puri, samosa) — slow digestion
- Large meals (>600 kcal) — diverts blood to digestion
- High-fiber foods (salad, sprouts) — may cause GI distress
- Spicy food (mirchi, schezwan) — heartburn during exercise
- Milk-heavy items if lactose sensitive — bloating
- Carbonated drinks — gas and discomfort

═══════════════════════════════════════
INTRA-WORKOUT NUTRITION
═══════════════════════════════════════

FOR WORKOUTS <60 MINUTES:
- Water only (150-250ml every 15-20 minutes)
- No food needed

FOR WORKOUTS 60-90 MINUTES:
- Water + electrolytes (pinch of salt + lemon in water)
- Optional: 5g BCAA if training fasted

FOR WORKOUTS >90 MINUTES (Endurance/Intense):
- 30-60g fast carbs per hour
- Options: Sports drink, coconut water + honey, dates
- Electrolyte drink: 500ml water + 1/4 tsp salt + 2 tbsp honey + lemon

HYDRATION DURING TRAINING:
- Sip 150-250ml every 15-20 minutes
- Don't gulp large amounts (stomach sloshing)
- Urine should be pale yellow post-workout
- Dehydration of just 2% bodyweight = 10-20% performance drop

═══════════════════════════════════════
POST-WORKOUT NUTRITION
═══════════════════════════════════════

THE RECOVERY WINDOW (0-2 Hours):
- Muscle protein synthesis (MPS) is elevated 50-100% for 24-48 hours
- The "30-minute anabolic window" is overstated but eating sooner is still better
- Glycogen replenishment is fastest in first 2 hours post-training
- Cortisol (catabolic hormone) drops faster with post-workout nutrition

POST-WORKOUT MACROS:
For Muscle Gain:
- Protein: 0.3-0.5g per kg bodyweight (25-40g)
- Carbs: 0.5-1.0g per kg bodyweight (40-80g)
- Fat: Low-moderate (doesn't slow protein absorption as once believed)
- Total: 400-700 kcal

For Fat Loss:
- Protein: 0.4-0.5g per kg bodyweight (30-40g)
- Carbs: 0.3-0.5g per kg bodyweight (20-40g)
- Fat: Low
- Total: 250-400 kcal

For Endurance:
- Carbs: 1.0-1.5g per kg (primary focus)
- Protein: 0.3g per kg
- Hydration: Critical — rehydrate with electrolytes

IMMEDIATE POST-WORKOUT (Within 30 min — Liquid/Quick):
- Whey protein (1 scoop) + banana: ~225 kcal, 26g P
- Whey shake + milk (200ml): ~250 kcal, 30g P
- Chocolate milk (300ml): ~250 kcal, 10g P (fast carb + protein)
- Fruit smoothie + whey: ~300 kcal, 28g P
- Banana + peanut butter (1 tbsp) + milk: ~300 kcal, 12g P
- Coconut water + whey protein: ~200 kcal, 25g P

FULL POST-WORKOUT MEAL (Within 1-2 Hours):

Non-Vegetarian:
- Chicken breast (150g) + rice (1.5 cups) + vegetables: ~550 kcal, 38g P
- Fish curry (light) + rice (1 cup): ~400 kcal, 26g P
- Egg bhurji (4 eggs) + 2 roti + fruit: ~520 kcal, 26g P
- Grilled chicken (150g) + sweet potato (200g): ~400 kcal, 34g P
- Chicken tikka (150g) + jeera rice (1 cup): ~470 kcal, 34g P
- Tandoori chicken (2 pieces) + 1 roti + salad: ~420 kcal, 36g P
- Tuna sandwich (whole wheat, 2 slices): ~350 kcal, 28g P
- Chicken pulao (1 plate) + raita: ~480 kcal, 24g P

Vegetarian:
- Paneer bhurji (150g) + 2 roti: ~540 kcal, 24g P
- Whey shake + oats + banana + peanut butter: ~450 kcal, 32g P
- Soy chunks curry (100g dry) + rice: ~500 kcal, 28g P
- Rajma (1 bowl) + rice (1.5 cups) + curd: ~550 kcal, 20g P
- Greek yogurt (200g) + granola + honey + banana: ~400 kcal, 22g P
- Tofu stir-fry (200g) + brown rice: ~400 kcal, 20g P
- Dal (1 bowl) + rice + paneer (50g): ~500 kcal, 22g P
- Chole (1 bowl) + 2 roti + chaas: ~520 kcal, 18g P

═══════════════════════════════════════
TRAINING TIME VARIATIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════

MORNING GYM (5-7 AM):
Schedule:
- 5:30 AM: Banana + black coffee (or train fasted for cardio)
- 6:00-7:00 AM: Workout
- 7:15 AM: Whey shake + banana (immediate)
- 8:30 AM: Full breakfast — 3 eggs + 2 roti + curd

For Fasted Morning Training:
- Acceptable for light cardio / fat loss focus
- NOT recommended for heavy strength training
- Minimum: 1 banana + coffee before lifting
- Post-workout meal becomes critical — don't delay

AFTERNOON GYM (12-2 PM):
Schedule:
- 10:00 AM: Solid breakfast (oats + eggs + fruit)
- 11:30 AM: Light snack (banana + almonds)
- 12:30-1:30 PM: Workout
- 2:00 PM: Whey shake
- 3:00 PM: Full lunch (chicken + rice + dal)

EVENING GYM (5-7 PM) — Most Common in India:
Schedule:
- 1:00 PM: Full lunch (largest meal)
- 4:30 PM: Pre-workout snack (banana + peanut butter)
- 5:30-7:00 PM: Workout
- 7:15 PM: Whey shake (optional)
- 8:00 PM: Dinner (chicken/paneer + roti + sabzi)

LATE NIGHT GYM (8-10 PM):
Schedule:
- 6:00 PM: Pre-workout meal (2 roti + dal + sabzi)
- 8:00-9:30 PM: Workout
- 10:00 PM: Post-workout meal (lighter — fish/eggs + 1 roti)
- Note: Don't skip post-workout even if late — recovery matters

SUPPLEMENTS — EVIDENCE-BASED GUIDE
═══════════════════════════════════════

PRE-WORKOUT SUPPLEMENTS:

Caffeine (STRONG evidence):
- Dose: 3-6mg per kg bodyweight (200-400mg for most)
- Timing: 30-60 minutes before training
- Source: Black coffee (95mg per cup), green tea (50mg), caffeine pills
- Benefits: +5-8% strength, +12% endurance, increased focus
- Don't consume after 4 PM (disrupts sleep)

Creatine Monohydrate (STRONGEST evidence):
- Dose: 3-5g daily (no loading needed, no cycling needed)
- Timing: Any time — consistency matters, not timing
- Benefits: +5-10% strength, +1-2 kg lean mass in 4-12 weeks
- Cheapest and most researched supplement. ₹500-800 for 60 servings.
- Safe for kidneys in healthy individuals (100+ studies confirm)

Beta-Alanine (Moderate evidence):
- Dose: 3.2-6.4g daily
- Benefits: Delays muscle fatigue in 1-4 minute efforts
- Side effect: Tingling/itching (harmless, subsides with use)

POST-WORKOUT SUPPLEMENTS:

Whey Protein (STRONG evidence — if diet is insufficient):
- Dose: 25-40g (1-1.5 scoops)
- Timing: Within 2 hours post-workout
- Types: Concentrate (80% protein, cheapest), Isolate (90%, less lactose), Hydrolysate (fastest, most expensive)
- Indian brands: MuscleBlaze, Optimum Nutrition, MyProtein

NOT NECESSARY:
- BCAAs: Redundant if eating 1.6g+ protein/kg daily
- Glutamine: No significant benefit for muscle gain in well-fed athletes
- Mass gainers: Just sugar + protein. Eat real food instead.
- Fat burners: Mostly caffeine + fillers. Save your money.
- Testosterone boosters: Zero OTC supplement meaningfully raises T levels

═══════════════════════════════════════
COMMON PRE/POST WORKOUT MISTAKES
═══════════════════════════════════════

PRE-WORKOUT MISTAKES:
- Training on empty stomach for strength work (low energy, higher injury risk)
- Eating too close to workout (<30 min, heavy meal): nausea
- Too much fat/fiber pre-workout: slow digestion, stomach issues
- Relying on pre-workout powder instead of real food
- Not hydrating: even 2% dehydration = 10-20% performance drop

POST-WORKOUT MISTAKES:
- Skipping post-workout meal: missed recovery window
- Only protein, no carbs: incomplete glycogen replenishment
- Waiting 3+ hours to eat: diminished recovery benefit
- Choosing slow-digesting proteins immediately after (casein, paneer)
  → Use whey or chicken first, save paneer for bedtime
- "I'll eat tomorrow" mentality: muscles repair NOW
- Drinking alcohol post-workout: suppresses MPS by 37% (one study)

═══════════════════════════════════════
HYDRATION PROTOCOL
═══════════════════════════════════════

PRE-WORKOUT: 400-600ml water, 30 min before
DURING: 150-250ml every 15-20 min
POST-WORKOUT: 500-750ml within 1 hour
DAILY: 35ml per kg bodyweight minimum

ELECTROLYTE NEEDS (for >60 min training or heavy sweating):
- Sodium: 300-600mg per hour of training
- Potassium: From food (banana, coconut water)
- DIY electrolyte drink: 500ml water + 1/4 tsp salt + 2 tbsp honey + lemon juice

SIGNS OF DEHYDRATION:
- Dark yellow urine
- Headache during/after training
- Muscle cramps
- Dizziness
- Decreased performance
